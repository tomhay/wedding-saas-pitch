## 1. How the Pricing Chain Works

Every product follows the same chain:

```
Step 1: Oshom net price        (what Oshom keeps after tax)
Step 2: × 1.21                 (Oshom's 21% tax & service charge)
Step 3: = BUY price            (what BaliLove pays Oshom)
Step 4: ÷ 0.85                 (add BaliLove's 15% commission)
Step 5: = SELL price            (what the couple pays)
```

**Total markup on Oshom net: × 1.4235 (42.35%)**

Example: If Oshom nets 1,000,000 → Buy = 1,210,000 → Sell = 1,423,529

## 5. The Core Tension

We need a price that satisfies three parties:

| Party | What They Want |
|-------|---------------|
| **Daisy (Oshom)** | At or above her 2026 budget ADR of 4,028,398 /room/night |
| **Selina (Sales)** | Guest room rate at or below 3,500,000 /room/night sell |
| **Couple** | Total out-of-pocket comparable to or better than Vedas (~217M - 265M) |

**Solution: Decouple the buyout rate from the guest room rate.**

- Buyout rate = what Oshom receives per room (based on ADR) → **satisfies Daisy**
- Guest room rate = what guests pay per room (based on market) → **satisfies Selina**
- The gap between the two = the couple's "exclusivity premium" → **keeps couple competitive with Vedas**

## 15. Resolved & Remaining Questions

### Resolved

1. **Venue fee** — 40M buy (incl tax). Goes to Oshom via the standard chain (net + 21% tax + 15% BL). This is a **discount lever** — can be reduced or waived to close a sale on off-peak dates.

2. **BYO alcohol / Corkage** — No corkage. Too small an amount to fight over. Guests can bring their own and we'll serve it, but with a reasonable limit. The cash bar is the primary model — guests buy drinks at Oshom's bar prices.

3. **Non-wedding day F&B** — Cash bar for guests by default. Aim to upsell functions (welcome dinner, recovery brunch) but no mandatory F&B on non-event days. Cash bar revenue goes to Oshom directly.

4. **Guest room rate discounting** — We'd suggest to Daisy that off-peak dates should have discounted guest room rates. Look at which months are struggling (Nov, Apr, Mar) and price guest rooms lower to drive bookings. This makes weddings more attractive in weak months and helps fill the calendar.

### Still Open

1. **Guest room rate of 3,500,000 sell** — is this the standard rate to present, with discounting available for off-peak? Or should we set a range (e.g. 2,500,000 - 3,500,000 depending on season)?

2. **F&B for non-wedding days in a 3-night package** — do we price welcome dinner / recovery brunch separately, or include a simple option in the package? Currently only the main wedding day F&B is costed.

3. **BYO limit** — what's reasonable? E.g. "up to 5 bottles of spirits" or "enough for the bridal party"? Need to agree something that doesn't undercut Oshom's bar revenue significantly.

4. **Minimum F&B spend** — we've said "no minimum" as a selling point, but internally the target is ~40M for wedding day, ~20M for other days. Do we communicate this to Daisy as a guideline, or keep it truly open?

### Key Changes vs Sections 6–14

1. **Event fee rolled into buyout** — no separate venue charge (Selina's ask)
2. **Price set by working backwards** from couple's total wedding cost in AUD
3. **Buyout rate is the variable** — F&B stays at Oshom's published menu prices

### Method

```
Couple total (AUD) × 10,600              = Total IDR
Total IDR − BL vendor services (189M)    = Couple venue OOP
Couple venue OOP + guest offset (119M)   = Venue sell (buyout + F&B)
Venue sell − F&B sell                     = Buyout sell (event fee rolled in)
Buyout sell × 0.85                        = Buyout buy (incl 21% tax)
Buyout buy ÷ 1.21                         = Oshom net (buyout)
Oshom net ÷ 18 rooms ÷ 2 nights          = Net per room per night
```

### 16f. The Volume Argument — Why Start at $35K

The $35K price point isn't about leaving money on the table. It's about speed to volume. **Give us control of pricing and the sales process, and we'll have 20 weddings booked by end of May.**

#### The pitch to Daisy:

> "How would you feel if you had 20 weddings booked by the end of May? Give us full control of the pricing and how we sell it. Our team has 300+ weddings of data — we know exactly who's buying what, how to overcome every objection, and we will work up the total revenue and profit to your business over time."

#### The condition: BL controls pricing and sales

This only works if BaliLove has **full autonomy** over:
- **Pricing** — we set the couple's total, the F&B tier options, and the seasonal adjustments
- **Sales process** — we handle leads end-to-end (enquiry → site visit → close → coordination)
- **Upselling** — we decide when and how to push Premium F&B, extra nights, vendor upgrades

Oshom's role: deliver the product (rooms, food, service). BL's role: fill the calendar and maximise revenue per wedding.

#### Why 20 weddings at $35K beats 10 weddings at $43K:

Mix scaled proportionally: 20 wed = 13×2-night + 7×3-night; 10 wed = 7×2-night + 3×3-night.

| Metric | 20 weddings @ $35K | 10 weddings @ $43K |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Wedding days used | 47 (13% of calendar) | 23 (6% of calendar) |
| Oshom revenue (through BL) | ~5,256,000,000 | ~3,334,000,000 |
| Displaced revenue | −2,073,000,000 | −1,015,000,000 |
| Net incremental | **+3,183,000,000** | **+2,319,000,000** |
| BL commission | ~928,000,000 | ~588,000,000 |
| Guest in-resort spend | ~473,000,000 | ~228,000,000 |

**$35K at 20 weddings delivers 37% more net revenue than $43K at 10 weddings** — using only 13% of the calendar vs 6%.

#### Why lower price = more weddings:

1. **70% of leads budget $30–35K** — at $35K we capture the mass market, not just the top 30%
2. **Beats every named competitor** — cheaper than Vedas ($38K), comparable to BBG ($36K), with 18 rooms vs 8
3. **BL sales team knows the objections** — 300+ weddings of experience closing deals. Lower price = fewer objections = faster close rate
4. **Conversion rate scales non-linearly** — dropping from $43K to $35K (−19%) could double or triple conversion from the same lead pool
5. **Speed matters** — 20 bookings by end of May means Oshom sees revenue impact in 2026, not 2027

#### The growth path:

| Phase | Timeline | Target | Price Strategy | Revenue Lever |
|-------|----------|--------|----------------|---------------|
| Launch | Now → May 2026 | 20 bookings | $35K base | Prove the model, fill calendar fast |
| Scale | Jun → Dec 2026 | 35–40 total | $35K off-peak, $40K peak | Season-based pricing, F&B upsells |
| Optimise | 2027+ | 50+ per year | Dynamic pricing | Full revenue optimization, marketplace |

**Phase 1 is about proof of concept and speed.** Get 20 weddings booked fast, deliver exceptional service, build a portfolio of real Oshom weddings. The revenue optimisation comes in Phase 2 once we have:
- Actual booking data (which months convert, which F&B tiers sell, which vendors add value)
- Testimonials and photos from real Oshom weddings
- Social proof to justify premium pricing on peak dates
- Data-backed confidence to push the average wedding value up from $35K toward $40K+
