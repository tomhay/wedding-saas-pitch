# Oshom Pricing: ADR Target vs Vedas Comparison

> The question: If we price Oshom at Daisy's 2026 ADR target (4.03M net/room), how does the couple's price compare to what they'd pay at Vedas?

---

## The Two-Sided Problem

The per-room rate matters twice:

1. **Oshom side:** Daisy wants at/above her ADR → she wants it HIGH
2. **Couple/guest side:** Selina says 3.5M sell is the ceiling → market wants it LOW

At Oshom's ADR of 4.03M net:
- Buy = 4.03M × 1.21 = **4,876,000 per room/night** (what we pay Oshom)
- Sell = 4,876,000 / 0.85 = **5,737,000 per room/night** (what couple/guest pays)

**5.74M/room/night sell is way above Selina's 3.5M ceiling.**

But — the guest doesn't necessarily see the per-room rate. They see what they're asked to pay for their room. We can set the guest room rate separately from the buyout math.

## Head-to-Head: Oshom (ADR) vs Vedas

### At 4.03M ADR (Oshom's target)

| | Oshom (2-night, Standard) | Vedas (2-night, Mid Season) |
|--|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| Rooms included | **18** | 8 |
| Total sell | 372,786,000 ($35,170) | ~292,200,000 ($27,570) |
| Guest room rate (sell) | 5,737,000/night | ~4,700,000/night |
| Guest rooms available for offset | 17 | 8 (max) |
| Max guest offset (2 nights) | 195,058,000 | ~75,200,000 |
| **Couple out-of-pocket** | **$16,770** | **~$20,000-25,000** |
| All guests on site? | **Yes (18 rooms)** | No (8 rooms, rest go elsewhere) |

**Interesting:** Even at the higher total price, Oshom's couple out-of-pocket could be LOWER than Vedas because 17 rooms of guest revenue offset the buyout vs only 8 at Vedas.

**BUT** — the guest room rate of 5.74M/night is above Selina's ceiling. If guests won't pay that, they won't book, and the offset doesn't happen.

### What if we cap the guest room rate at 3.5M sell?

Guests pay 3.5M/night. Couple covers the gap.

| | Oshom (guest rate 3.5M) | Oshom (guest rate 5.74M) |
|--|------------------------|------------------------|
| Guest offset (17 × 3.5M × 2) | 119,000,000 | 195,058,000 |
| Couple out-of-pocket | 253,786,000 ($23,940) | 177,728,000 ($16,770) |

At 3.5M guest rate, couple's out-of-pocket rises to ~$24K — now it's comparable to Vedas.

## My Recommendation

**Go with Scenario B: Full ADR buyout, capped guest rate at 3.5M.**

Here's why:

1. **Daisy gets her ADR** — 4.03M net/room. She can't argue with that. She's getting exactly what she budgeted, at 100% occupancy instead of 61%.

2. **Guests pay a rate the market accepts** — 3.5M sell/room/night. At or just below Selina's ceiling.

3. **Couple's out-of-pocket (~$24K AUD)** is in the same ballpark as Vedas — but they get a FULL HOTEL (18 rooms, all guests on-site) vs Vedas' 8 rooms.

4. **The pitch to the couple:** "For similar money to a villa wedding, you get a private luxury hotel where ALL your guests stay together. No splitting across Bali. No coordinating transport."

5. **The pitch to Daisy:** "We're paying your budget ADR. Every room, every night. 100% guaranteed occupancy on wedding dates vs hoping for 61%."

### The gap between buyout rate and guest rate is the couple's "premium for exclusivity"

- Buyout sell rate per room/night: 5,737,000
- Guest rate per room/night: 3,500,000
- Gap: 2,237,000/room/night
- Total gap (17 rooms × 2 nights): 76,058,000 (~$7,175 AUD)

This is what the couple pays for the privilege of having the whole hotel to themselves. That's fair — and it's less than the venue fee at most other places.

## Remaining Question

**Should the guest room rate include the 15% BL commission, or is it passed through at cost?**

- Option 1: Guest pays 3.5M sell → BL keeps 15% (525K) → Oshom gets 2,975,000 → net after tax 2,459,000. But this means the room revenue going to offset the buyout is only 2.975M, not 3.5M. The couple's buyout obligation reduces by less.

- Option 2: Guest pays 3.5M → all goes to reduce the couple's buyout balance → BL commission is already built into the buyout sell price.

**Option 2 makes more sense** — BL's commission is on the overall package, not double-dipping on guest rooms. The rooms are just a payment mechanism to reduce the couple's cost.
