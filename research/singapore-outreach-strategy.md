### 3. Schedule Setup
- Book 3-4 meetings per day max (morning, lunch, afternoon, drinks)
- Leave gaps — meetings run over, people refer you to someone else same-day
- Book 1 dinner per night with a warm contact
- Base yourself at a members' club (1880 or Straits Clan) for credibility + spontaneous meetings

## During The Trip

### Day 1-2: MFO Meetings
Multi-family offices first. They're gatekeepers to 10-30 families each. One good MFO meeting = 5 investor intros.

**Pitch flow (30 min):**
1. (2 min) Who you are — 7 years, 300+ weddings, 64 staff
2. (3 min) The opportunity — you pay venue owners $4M/yr, now buying the venues
3. (5 min) Walk through love-os.com/fund on your laptop
4. (5 min) Property pipeline — A, B, C with economics
5. (5 min) The vision — Aman for weddings, YSG design partner, AI platform
6. (5 min) Terms + returns
7. (5 min) Q&A — expect: "first-time fund manager?", "what if tourism drops?", "exit?"

**Leave behind:** Business card + "deck is at love-os.com/fund, I'll send the password"

### Day 3-4: SFO / Individual Meetings
More personal, longer meetings. These are decision-makers, not intermediaries.

- Bring your laptop with the deck loaded
- Have Bali property photos on your phone
- Offer a Bali site visit — "come see the properties, meet the team"
- Be ready to talk specific deal terms if they're interested on the spot

### Day 5: Follow-ups + Overflow
- Send thank-you notes same day as each meeting
- Follow up on any intros received during the week
- Take overflow meetings from referrals

## Post-Trip

### Within 48 hours:
- Send password + deck link to everyone you met
- Personalised follow-up email referencing specific conversation points
- Send Bali site visit invitation with proposed dates

### Within 2 weeks:
- Follow up on any MFO intros to their clients
- Schedule Bali site visits for interested parties
- Begin NDA process for anyone wanting audited financials

### Ongoing:
- Monthly update email to all contacts (brief, 3-4 bullet points on progress)
- Close expressions of interest before lawyer finalises vehicle structure

## Key Talking Points

**When they ask "why should I trust a first-time fund manager?"**
> "I'm not a fund manager who discovered Bali. I'm a 7-year Bali operator who's raising capital for properties I already fill. 268 weddings booked. The demand exists before the capital is deployed."

**When they ask "what's the downside?"**
> "Even in our downside, you collect guaranteed lease income on freehold land appreciating 8-13% annually. The 12% compounding hurdle means I earn nothing until you've made 2.2x. And you own freehold real estate in Bali — the property has intrinsic value completely independent of the operator."

**When they ask "why not just get bank financing?"**
> "We will — that's the compounding strategy. Properties are cash-flow positive from day one, which enables debt. Your equity gets levered into a larger portfolio. We want investor partners first because we're building something bigger than one deal."

**When they ask "why Singapore?"**
> "Singapore is the capital hub for SEA hospitality. Your family offices understand Indonesian property, PMA structures, and Bali tourism economics. And you're 2.5 hours from seeing the properties in person."
